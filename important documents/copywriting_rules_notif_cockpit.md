# Copywriting Rules: The Notification Cockpit

These rules define the "Modern Bureaucrat" persona—a bridge between official, legally-binding recruitment notifications (AIIMS, UPSC, SSC, RRB) and an easy-to-use, supportive administrative interface.

---

## 🏗️ 1. The Nomenclature Hierarchy

Every UI element must follow this two-layer hierarchy:

1.  **Primary Header (Official Document Accuracy)**: The main label MUST be a 1:1 match with official recruitment notification PDFs. 
    *   *Example*: Use **"Relaxation in Upper Age Limit"** instead of "Category Bonuses."
    *   *Why*: Administrators cross-reference their work against official PDFs. Zero-ambiguity saves jobs.

2.  **Sub-text / Caption (Conversational Context)**: The secondary text should be conversational, explaining *how* the rule helps the admin or the candidate.
    *   *Example*: "Applying age benefits for reserved categories ensures an inclusive recruitment process."

---

## 📜 2. Strategic Rules

### RULE 1: Direct Matching
If a term exists in an official gazette, use that term. Never simplify "Ex-Servicemen" to "Veterans" or "Cut-off Date" to "Finish Line."
- **Standardized Terms**: ESM (Ex-Servicemen), Age as on (Cut-off Date), Reservation Category, Age Relaxation, Qualifications, Academic & Clinical Routes.

### RULE 2: Direct Professionalism
Avoid passive voice. Use assertive, task-oriented verbs that focus on the action of "saving the job."
- **DO**: *"Set the primary age boundary."*
- **DON'T**: *"This is where you can change the age limits."*

### RULE 3: Supportive Emotional Anchors
While the headers are formal, use helper-text to lead from "emotion angles." Frame rules as "support" or "benefits" rather than "restrictions."
- **DO**: *"Supporting our veterans by applying service-year deductions."*
- **DON'T**: *"Subtract military years from age."*

### RULE 4: No Marketing Decoration
Avoid "SaaS-speak" and fluffy adjectives (e.g., "fast," "easy," "intelligent"). An institutional tool earns trust through precision, not marketing.
- **NEVER** use: "Easy age calculator," "Powerful eligibility tool."

### RULE 5: Explicit Toggles
Toggles must clearly state what is being "Enabled" or "Enforced." 
- **DO**: *"Enforce Marital Inclusion Gate?"*
- **DON'T**: *"Active?"*

---

## 🚦 3. Checklist for Future Overhauls

When refactoring a section (Identity, Quals, Job Type), ask:
1.  **PDF Match**: Does this header exist in a standard AIIMS/SSC notification?
2.  **Clarity**: If I remove the sub-text, is the legal rule still obvious?
3.  **Humanity**: If a new teammate comes in, does the helper-text explain the *positive impact*?
4.  **Density**: Is the high-fidelity nomenclature supported by a high-density "Cockpit" layout?
